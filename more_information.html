Who are we and how does our scheme work

We are a non-profit organisation that is passionate about helping the next generation of STEM students. Tutoring is principally an expensive luxury, only affordable and accessible to a subset of those that could benefit from it. That is why we make tutoring free, which we are able to do through voluntary tutors.

Tutoring in-person also has its limitations: namely the need to have tutors geographically nearby, and the need to travel for tutors or tutees. Both of these alone often mean that tutoring is impractical if not impossible. By providing tutoring online through the use of video call applications with the ability to screen share, we make tutoring effortless and convenient for both tutor and tutee, and thus enable those from different locations throughout the UK to connect.

Who are our tutors

Our tutors can be anyone that has evidenced expertise in one or more STEM subjects. We hope to attract individuals seeking volunteering opportunities in order to gain valuable skills and experience. Furthermore we hope to provide an insight to those thinking of possibly entering the teaching profession.

The bulk of our tutors tend to always be STEM undergraduates , however many of our tutors are also retired teachers or STEM professionals, and in general include any individuals that are keen to offer their expertise and time in assisting students in need of help with their studies.

Why be a tutor

By becoming a tutor, you are providing a student with an invaluable service, one which ordinarily they could not access and or afford. By receiving dedicated 1-to-1 assistance on a topic or problem tailored to their needs, a student can thrive, in a STEM subject, paving the way for them to succeed in it academically and also pursue a degree and or career in the subject as an adult. Further to this, such a positive learning experience with a tutor skilled in a STEM subject, can inspire a student to pursue a STEM career.

Tutoring offers tutors a great opportunity to develop their professional skills and qualities, and will also give them great satisfaction in being able to help students solve their problems.

How do the tutor sessions work

Tutors register via the tutor application form, and indicate when each week and in what subjects they will be available to tutor. Students can, after consulting the tutor availability timetable, book a session at least 48hrs in advance, by submitting the book a session form.

In the form the student will provide a short description of the topic or problem in a STEM subject they wish to discuss with a tutor. They will then receive within 24hrs an email confirming whether or not a tutor is available. A suitable available tutor will then be notified of a booking, and be informed of the topic the student wants to cover. On the day of the tutoring session the tutor will need to arrange a video call, and the tutee will be emailed an invitation link to join. The tutor will then commence their prepared session which will last for approximately a maximum of one hour.
